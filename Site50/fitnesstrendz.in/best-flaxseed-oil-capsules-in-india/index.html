What is Flaxseed Oil

Flaxseeds oil comes from the seeds of the flax plant. It contains both Omega 3 and Omega 6 which are needed for good health. There are so many other sources of omega 3 such as chia seeds, walnuts, soybean oil, eggs, yoghurt, juices, milk and soy beverages, etc.

The three main omega-3 fatty acids are alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA), and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA). ALA is found mainly in plant oils such as flaxseed, soybean, they all are highly important components of the membranes that surround each cell in your body.

Flaxseed is the richest vegetable source of lignans. Lignans are basically a group of chemical that plays a role in cancer prevention. Flaxseed oil is available in liquid and soft gel capsule forms. FAQs

How do omega-3s differ from omega-6s?
A key difference between these fatty acids is where they are found. Omega-3s come primarily from cold-water fatty fish while omega-6s are found in plant oils, including corn, soybean, and sunflower oils

How long will Flax Seed store?
lax Seed in the whole seed form will store easily for over two years. It does not need to be refrigerated or frozen, just store it in a food storage container out of the sunlight. 

How does Flaxseed capsules work?
Flaxseed oil is a source of polyunsaturated fatty acids such as alpha-linolenic acid. The alpha-linolenic acid and related chemicals in flaxseed oil seem to decrease inflammation. That is why flaxseed oil is thought to be useful for rheumatoid arthritis and another inflammatory (swelling) diseases.
