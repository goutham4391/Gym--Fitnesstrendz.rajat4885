What is Green Tea

As per the research, tea is the second most consumed beverage in the world, after water. Green tea is originated from the plant named Camellia Sinensis. It is made by lightly steaming leaves from the Camellia Sinensis plant, which is the same plant used to make black and oolong teas.

Green tea is primarily water-based and the best part about green tea is that It doesn’t contain any fat, carbohydrates, or protein, and there aren’t any calories in unsweetened tea. It means it’s free of the usual macronutrients found in other foods and drinks.

Green tea is one of the least oxidized tea types. It is made from young tea leaves plucked towards the close of the flush or harvest season after the production of white, oolong, and black teas. Here are some great health benefits of green tea mention below.

Benefits of Green Tea

1. Increased Mental Alertness

Green tea extracts contain good amount of caffeine which helps you in mental focus and increased mental alertness. It is also one of the best pre-workout which you can use because all the pre-workout available in the market contains caffeine as their primary ingredient.

2. Prevent Heart Disease 

Green tea helps lower high blood pressure which is the main reason for heart problems. It is also beneficial to lower levels of the bad kind of cholesterol, thereby reducing the risk of developing heart disease.

3. Improve Brain Function

The key element in green tea is coffee which improves various aspects of brain function, but caffeine isn’t the only brain-boosting compound in green tea. It also contains L-theanine amino acids that increase the activity of the inhibitory neurotransmitter GABA, which has anti-anxiety effects.

4. Natural Fat Burner

Green tea also works as a fat burner, if you check any fat burner supplement, you will surely found green tea extracts there. It is because green tea is ideal to boost the metabolic rate in our body and improve physical performance by mobilizing fatty acids from fat tissue and making them available for use as energy.

5. Lower the risk of Cancer

The main reason for cancer is due to uncontrolled growth of cells. As we know that green tea contains anti-oxidant that can help protect against oxidative damage. Many studies indicate that green tea drinkers are less likely to develop several types of cancer.

6. Reduce Bad Breath

Green tea is also beneficial for oral teeth. The catechins in green tea may inhibit the growth of bacteria in the mouth, reducing the risk of bad breath. It also prevents you from plaque formation, cavities and tooth decay.

7. Helpful in Type 2 Diabetes

Diabetes is one of the major problems around the world these days. Many studies have shown that regular use of green tea and help you prevent type 2 diabetes. One study indicates that those who drank more green tea had an approximately 42% lower risk of type 2 diabetes.

8. Prevent cardiovascular disease

We all know that cardiovascular diseases such as heart disease and stroke, are the leading causes of death worldwide. Green tea improves the total cholesterol level in the body and increases the antioxidant capacity of the blood, which blocks the pathway of heart disease.

9. Help in lose weight

As I mentioned above that green tea can boost the metabolic rate in the short term which can directly help you lose weight. Apart from this, it has 0 calories so there will be no effect on your diet and you can use it to reduce body fat, especially from the abdominal area.

10. Reduce Arthritis Symptoms

Inflammation is also a major problem due to an inappropriate diet schedule. If you’re looking to decrease inflammation, add green tea to your shopping list. Apart from this, it will reduce the chances of arthritis as well.

FAQs

Is green tea good for health?
Yes, it is. Modern-day research shows that regular consumption of green tea is known to reduce cholesterol, aid in burning fat and reducing cardiovascular disease and cancer.

How to identify a good green tea?
After you have brewed the green tea, the tea leaves should have their original character. This means that the tea leaves should turn green, you can take a leaf and chew it to see if it is bitter. Good green tea will have no after bitter taste.

What are the cons of drinking green tea?
Two to three cups of green tea is fine but as you know an excessive dose of anything will have a negative impact so the Side effects of drinking green tea may include “nausea, vomiting, dehydration, lethargy and dizziness.

Does tea contain caffeine?
Yes, but a cup of tea contains far less caffeine than a cup of coffee. Tea also contains L-Theanine that helps counter the effects of caffeine.

Can drinking tea help one lose weight?
Drinking tea regularly can increase the metabolism of your body and give you the energy to live a more active lifestyle. This in turn can be of great help if you’re trying to lose weight.
