What is Oats?

Oats are annual grasses and are used both for human and animal nutrition, in addition to other purposes. They are a commonly grown crop in the world today. Before being used as human food, they were used to feed horses and for medicinal purposes.

Oat is a widely known cereal grain and used for animal feed and as human food because of its nutritional value and added health benefits. Oats were recognized as a healthy food in the mid-1980s and then they become more popular for human nutrition.

Oats contain more soluble fibre than any other grain. Soluble fibre is the kind that dissolves in water, so the body turns it into a kind of thick, viscous gel, which moves very slowly through your body. One of the benefits is that your stomach stays fuller longer, providing satiety. Soluble fibre also slows the absorption of glucose into the body, which means you’re going to avoid those nasty sugar highs and lows.

Correct method to make oats

Method 1

  • Bring 1 cup of water or milk to a boil in a small saucepan.
  • Stir in 1/2 cup of oats and reduce heat to medium
  • Add a pinch of salt or a spoon of Organic Honey, stir occasionally, for 5 minutes.
  • Remove from heat. Cover and let it stand for 2 to 3 minutes.
  • If using a microwave combine 1 cup water or milk with Oats 1/2 cup and a pinch of salt.

Method 2

  • Add 1 scoop of oats to a bowl
  • Add 1 cup hot want in it and cover it with a lid for almost 10 minutes
  • Now, add half scoop of whey protein and pinch cinnamon powder to add flavour, mix it well
  • Add some peanuts, almonds and cashews
  • Now, store it in a fridge for about 5 minutes and it’s ready.

Is oats good for skin?
Oatmeal can soak up the excess oil on your skin and help treat acne. Its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties help to treat dry skin and remove dead skin cells.

Can I eat oats at night?
While many of us associate oats with breakfast time, they are also the perfect evening snack. Oats are a good natural source of melatonin, which is often taken as a sleep aid due to its ability to help regulate the body’s internal clock.

Can oats increase height?
